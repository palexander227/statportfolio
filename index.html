About Me

Many important decisions educators must make are informed by two largely antipodal viewpoints: that technology holds the key to transforming the educational experience and unleashing the power of young minds to subsequently revolutionize our 21st century world --- And (opposite, but equally wrong), that technology is greatly interfering with and distracting from the classical purposes of general education and should be avoided except where absolutely necessary. The truth, I believe, is most likely in the tension that holds these views in equilibrium. My primary purpose in researching and designing applications for use in education reflects that point of view. As an educator with many years of classroom experience, I am deeply convinced that it is the personal interactions with human instructors that most profoundly influence student orientation toward life-long learning. As a scientist, I also believe we can bring technology to bear to optimize the effectiveness of our teachers. Unless we really want our schools to be little more than "tech-trade" brokers, we must seize these opportunities.
